# Past Work

Crisp reference of every paper in `docs/papers/`. Max 2-3 headline numbers each — full detail lives in the paper, not here. CNN/channel-pruning papers (MetaPruning, Network Slimming) excluded on purpose. ⭐ = prunes a model we've also pruned (exact) or same family (related).

| Paper | Method (1 line) | Learned? | Dataset | Headline numbers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DISP-LLM** ⭐⭐ (Gao 2024) | Hypernetwork (GRU+FC) trained via gradient descent (STE/ReinMax) to set per-layer widths | **Learned** | WikiText-2 + Alpaca | OPT-125M: 27.65→22.95 ppl @20% pruned. LLaMA-7B 50% structured: 11.47 ppl (beats/matches 2:4 semi-structured SparseGPT's 11.00). *Same model (OPT-125M) + closest architectural analogue to ours (hypernetwork).* **Compute**: fixed 10,000 iterations/batch=1 *per (model, target-sparsity)*, regardless of difficulty — LLaMA-7B: 2.41hr/2×A100; LLaMA-13B: 8.83hr/4×A100; OPT-125M not stated. No separate orig-model forward pass per step (cheaper than ours per-sample), but batch=1 is likely poor GPU utilization — net FLOPs/wall-clock vs. ours genuinely unclear, not computed. |
| **SDS** ⭐⭐ (Li 2024) | Sparse→Dense→Sparse: reconstructs weights between two one-shot prune passes | Not learned | WikiText2 (eval), C4 (128-sample recon) | OPT-125M 2:4 sparse ppl: 27.66 (dense) → 60.43 (SparseGPT) → **27.94** (SDS, near-full recovery). *Same model (OPT-125M).* |
| **GISP** ⭐⭐ (Wang 2025) | Global (not per-layer) iterative pruning, first-order loss-based importance, block-normalized | Not learned | WikiText-2 + C4 (+GSM8K variant) | Iteration (32 steps) cuts 50%-sparsity ppl by **92.8%** relative to one-shot global pruning, Llama2-7B/C4. *Tests Llama3-8B AND Mistral-0.3-7B directly — our exact next targets.* |
| **SparseGPT** ⭐ (Frantar 2023) | One-shot layerwise sparse regression (approx. Optimal Brain Surgeon), closed-form weight update | Not learned | C4 calibration | 50-60% unstructured sparsity on OPT-175B/BLOOM-176B, minimal ppl increase; larger models more compressible. *OPT family, though headline is at the 175B end, not our 125M.* |
| **LLM-Pruner** ⭐ (Ma 2023) | One-shot gradient/Hessian importance + LoRA recovery (3hr, 50K samples) | Not learned | Eval: WikiText2 + zero-shot QA | 20% params removed → **94.97%** of original performance retained (LLaMA-7B/Vicuna-7B/ChatGLM-6B). *LLaMA family.* |
| **FLAP** ⭐ (An 2023) | Retraining-free: prunes via input-fluctuation stability metric + bias compensation | Not learned | WikiText-2 | LLaMA-7B, 50% pruned, no retrain: 12.62→**31.80** ppl (vs. LLM-Pruner's 130.97 without retrain). *LLaMA family.* |
| **SlimLLM** ⭐ (Guo 2025) | Calibration-based channel+head importance (Pearson similarity + activation direction/magnitude) + linear-regression repair | Not learned | LLaMA benchmark | LLaMA-7B, 20% pruned: **98.7%** of original performance retained. *LLaMA family.* |
| **Wanda** ⭐ (Sun 2024) | One-shot, prune by \|weight\|×‖activation‖₂ per output, single forward pass, no weight update | Not learned | C4 (128-seq calib), WikiText2 eval | Outperforms magnitude pruning by a large margin, rivals SparseGPT, **300× faster** metric computation. *LLaMA/LLaMA-2 family.* |
| **CoFi** (Xia 2022) | Jointly-learned coarse+fine L0 masks + layerwise distillation, task-specific | **Learned** | GLUE, SQuAD v1.1 | **12.1×** speedup at 95% of teacher's MNLI accuracy (84.8→80.6). *BERT-classification, different task/arch — no model overlap.* |
| **Ours** (LEP) | Trained BiLSTM hypernetwork (row-encoder + cross-layer context) scores FFN neurons, STE gates, frozen base | **Learned** | WikiText-2 | OPT-125M: free region to **25.66% pruned at +0.086 ppl** (23.941→24.027); real cost by 40%+ pruned (+2 to +5 ppl). *Weaker than DISP-LLM at matched sparsity (their 20%-pruned OPT-125M improves ppl; ours costs by 20-25%) and SDS (their 2:4/50% near-fully recovers dense; ours is +4.7 ppl at 41.6%) — honest gap, not glossed over.* **Compute**: convergence-based, adapts to difficulty — full 9-λ OPT-125M sweep (9 separate operating points, 500-950 steps each, batch=8) measured at **~52 min total** on real hardware. DISP-LLM pays a fixed 10,000-iteration budget per single point regardless — real methodological edge on adaptivity; not a confirmed net compute win (see their row). |
